Online vs live poker tracking

Serious players often mix online sessions with home games or casino trips. Keeping a single ledger with clear labels for where you played makes bankroll and hourly figures meaningful when you filter or review trends.

Why location type matters

Online volume, travel expenses, and live game dynamics differ. When your tracker records “online” vs “casino” vs “home,” you can answer questions like: whether your hourly is higher live, or how much of your volume is online — without merging unlike things into one vague bucket.

One bankroll story

Profit still flows from the same poker bankroll; the app’s job is to show that story honestly. Mixing everything without labels obscures where you are actually winning or need work.

Filters and groups

When you share results in a group, moderators can restrict what counts (for example online-only leaderboards). That only works if sessions were tagged consistently when logged.

Habits that help

Log sessions soon after play while memory is fresh: stakes, duration, net result, and site or room name. Small consistency beats rare mega-updates.
